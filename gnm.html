General Nursing and Midwifery

DETAILS OF THE PROGRAMME

The GNM program is a three-year diploma course that aims to provide students with the knowledge and skills required to provide quality nursing care to patients in various healthcare settings. The program combines theoretical and practical training in nursing, midwifery, and community health nursing.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

  1. Minimum education eligibility criteria for admission to GNM:
    • 10+2 with English having 40% of marks in Science/Arts/Commerce/Health science stream from a recognized CBSE board/State/Centre
    • Candidates are also eligible from state open School recognized by State Government and National Institute of Open School (NIOS) Recognized by Central Government having Science subjects and English only.
    • Registered ANM/LHV with pass marks
    • Sex: Female / Male
    • Knowledge of Konkani / Marathi is desirable.
  2. Reservation
    • For disabled candidates: 3% Disability reservation to be considered with a disability of locomotor t○ the tune of 40% to 50% of the lower extremity and other eligibility criteria with regard to age and qualification will be same as prescribed for each nursing programme

      • Note: A committee to be formed consisting of medical officer authorized by medical board of state government and a nursing expert in the panel which may decide whether the candidates have the disability of locomotor of 40% to 50%.

    • 5% of total marks is relaxed for SC/ST candidates
    • Any other reservation as per the State Govt.
    • Students shall be medically fit.
    • Minimum age for admission will be 17 years. (as on 31st December of that year) The upper age limit is 35yrs. For ANM/LHV, there is no age bar.

RULES FOR ADMISSION:

  1. Candidates seeking admission for this program should fill in and submit the application form attached to this Prospectus.
  2. Selection of the candidate will be done purely on merit

REQUIRED DOCUMENTS:

Applications should be accompanied by Self Attested photocopies of each of the following documents.

  1. Certificate of Birth from the Registrar.
  2. Certificate of passing and statement of marks of 12th Std. examination.
  3. School Leaving Certificate and certificate stating Medium of instruction.
  4. Medical fitness certificate from Authorized Medical Practitioner.
  5. In case of the applicant belonging to Schedule Caste /Schedule Tribe/Other Backward community of the State of Goa Certificate from Deputy Collector concerned to that effect.
  6. In case the applicant belongs to EWS of the state Goa, income and assets certificate from Mamlatdar of concerned taluka to that effect.
  7. In case of physically handicapped candidates a certificate from the constituted Medical Board of GMC Hospital Bambolim, Hospicio Hospital Margao and North Goa District (Asilo) Hospital Mapusa, regarding the nature and the extent to which the candidate is fit for pursuing the diploma (extend of disabilities should be 40% and above).
  8. Residence Certificate

ATTENDANCE

  • Students are expected to attend all classes, clinical rotations, and practical sessions as per the schedule provided by the institution. The minimum attendance required to be eligible for appearing in the final examination is 80% in theory & 100% in practical.
  • Latecomers who arrive after the start of the session will be marked absent for that day if they arrive more than 15 minutes after the start of the session. In case of absence due to illness or any other valid reason, students must produce a medical certificate or a letter explaining the reason for absence within 3 days of returning to college.
  • Any shortfall of practical attendance below 100% the student should make up in respective practical areas of absences before declaration of the result of final exams.

EXAMINATION

The institute will conduct two term examination in theory and practical for internal assessment during 1st , 2nd and 3rd year, as per the regulations by the Indian Nursing Council Passing marks will be 50% in each subject in practical as well as theory. The students must pass an internal examination to be eligible for the final examination conducted by Goa Nursing Council.

  1. Examination Pattern:
    • The examination pattern will consist of both theory and practical examinations.
    • The theory examination will consist of multiple-choice questions, short answer questions, and essay questions.
    • The practical examination will test the candidate's clinical skills and competence in various nursing procedures.
  2. Examination Eligibility:
    • Candidates must have a minimum attendance of 80% in theory & 100% in practical in each subject to be eligible for appearing in the final examination.
    • Candidates must have cleared all the internal examinations conducted by the institution.
  3. Examination Fees/ Internal examination fees
    • Candidates must pay the examination fees as per the GNC guidelines and the fee structure notified by the respective institution.
  4. Examination Results:
    • The results of the GNM examination will be declared by the Goa Nursing Council (GNC), and communicated to the respective institutions and candidates.
    • Candidates who have passed the examination will be awarded a GNM certificate by the Goa Nursing Council.
  5. Examination Malpractice:
    • Any form of examination malpractice, including copying, cheating, or impersonation, will be dealt with strictly as per the GNC guidelines and the rules of the institution.
    • Candidates found guilty of malpractice will be disqualified from the examination and will face disciplinary action.

INTERNAL ASSESSMENT

Will be done by the score obtained in curricular activities listed below.

  1. Nursing care plan
  2. Daily Diary
  3. Health-Talk
  4. Case Study
  5. Case Presentation
  6. Drug Study
  7. Process Recording
  8. Mental Status examination
  9. Observation Report
  10. Family Health Nursing care Plan
  11. Group Project
